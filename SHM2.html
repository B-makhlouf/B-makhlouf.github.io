Isotopic signatures to explore how patterns of salmon production have changed in Alaska's largest river basins

Understanding where fish are produced in large western Alaska watersheds is a challenge

In Alaska's largest watersheds; the Yukon and Kuskokwim River-basins, salmon migrate over large distances and through extremely remote ecosystems. Because of this, understanding the spatial distribution of salmon production is challenge. However, to effectively manage a mixed stock fishery requires understanding 1) which portions of the watershed contribute to the fishery and 2) how these patterns change over multiple temporal scales.

Otoliths can give us insight into how these patterns change over time

By harnessing a long term dataset of otoliths from the Yukon and Kuskokwim river basins, we will reconstruct spatial patterns of fishery production over several years. In doing so, we hope to uncover stock structures at a spatial scale currently unavailable, and contribute towards our understanding of the tradeoff between productivity and variability in salmon ecosystems.
