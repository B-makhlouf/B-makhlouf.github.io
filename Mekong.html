Otolith Sr87/86 to uncover unknown migration patterns in the Mekong River Basin

Can otoliths tell us where fish have gone in the Mekong River?

Many fish migration pathways in the Mekong river are currently unknown. However, the proliferation of hydroelectric dams in the Mekong River Basin has made it increasingly important to understand how connected waterways may be a part of life history strategies among fish.

Can otoliths help us uncover which species may be vulnerable to increased hydropower?

The Mekong River flows through geologically complex landscapes, which drives differences in Sr87/86 among different portions of the watershed. I am using these signals in fishes caught in Tonle Sap Lake to explore different life history strategies among fish species in the Mekong River Basin.
