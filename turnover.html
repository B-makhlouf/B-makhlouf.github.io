Confronting assumptions about incorporation of otolith Sr87/Sr86

Experimental testing to further understanding of change in otolith Sr8786 following movement

Many otolith based methods rely on key assumptions

Many otolith based methods rely on the key assumption that otolith Sr87/86 isotope ratios directly reflect that of their local environment. However, there may be cases in which this is not true. One such case is immediately following movement, where otolith Sr87/86 will equilibrate to the new water value over time. However, the rate at which this occurs is currently unknown.

Laboratory experiments give us a starting place to quantify these in wild fishes.

Through a controlled water switch experiment, we are assessing the rate and turnover dynamics of otolith Sr87/86 following movement between two unique environments. Values from this study may not directly reflect those of wild fishes, but give us a starting place to quantify these effects and consider them in applications of otolith-based movement recnstructions.
