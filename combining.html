Combining complementary data to improve basin scale provenance reconstructions

Multiple data sources resolve uncertainty and improve model precision

Current Methods face limitations on their own

Understanding spatial patterns of mobile species is important to designing effective management and climate adaptation strategies. However, this can be difficult in remote places or at large spatial scales. In pacific salmon, genetics and isotope-based techniques are two common methods to infer provenance. However, they both face serious limitations which limit their utility in many cases.

Can combining data sources from both methods improve provenance assignments?

Although genetics-based techniques and otolith-based isotope techniques have traditionally seen as alternative methods, they contain complementary datasets. We are exploring whether combining these data sources in a bayesian framework can resolve the limitations of each method alone, and improve population scale estimates of provenance of Pacific Salmon in large watersheds.
